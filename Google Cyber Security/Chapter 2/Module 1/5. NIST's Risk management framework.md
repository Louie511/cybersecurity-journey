## This lecture introduces the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Risk Management Framework (RMF), outlining its seven steps for managing security and privacy risks.

## NIST Risk Management Framework Overview

- The NIST RMF provides a structured approach for organizations to manage security and privacy risks.
- Understanding this framework is beneficial for entry-level analysts, even if they don't engage in every step.

## The Seven Steps of the RMF

### Prepare: 
- Involves activities to manage security and privacy risks before a breach, such as monitoring for risks and identifying controls.

### Categorize: 
- Focuses on developing risk management processes and tasks, considering the impact of risks on confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

### Select: 
- Choosing, customizing, and documenting controls to protect the organization, like maintaining a playbook.

### Implement: 
- Putting security and privacy plans into action to minimize the impact of ongoing risks.

### Assess: 
- Determining if established controls are implemented correctly and identifying potential weaknesses.

### Authorize: 
- Being accountable for security and privacy risks, which can involve generating reports and establishing project milestones.

### Monitor: 
- Continuously assessing and maintaining technical operations to ensure systems support the organization's security goals.
