## 🔷 focuses on the final elements of an internal security audit, building upon the initial planning stages.

## 🔺 Completing the Audit Elements

- Before finalizing the audit, it's crucial to review the scope, goals, and risk assessment to ensure all questions regarding audit objectives, asset risks, and control sufficiency are addressed.
- The remaining elements of an internal security audit include a controls assessment, compliance assessment, and communication of results.

## 🔺 Controls Assessment and Classification

- A controls assessment involves evaluating an organization's existing assets and potential risks to determine the effectiveness of internal controls and processes.
### Controls are typically classified into: 
- administrative (policies and procedures)
- technical (hardware and software solutions)
- physical (measures to prevent physical access) categories.
