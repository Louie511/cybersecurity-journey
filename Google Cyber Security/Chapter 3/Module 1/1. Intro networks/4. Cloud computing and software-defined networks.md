## 🔷 This lecture explores cloud computing and networking, including hybrid networks and software-defined networks, and their benefits for organizations.

## 🔺 Cloud Computing Fundamentals

### ⚫ On-premise vs. Cloud: 
- Traditional networks keep all devices at a company's physical location, while cloud computing uses remote servers and services hosted on the internet by Cloud Service Providers (CSPs).

### ⚫ Cloud Service Provider Offerings: 
- CSPs provide services like Software as a Service (SaaS) for remote software use, Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) for virtual computer components, and Platform as a Service (PaaS) for application development tools.

## 🔺 Hybrid and Software-Defined Networks

### ⚫ Hybrid Cloud Environments: 
- Organizations often use a combination of CSP services and their own on-premise resources, known as a hybrid cloud environment, or multi-cloud when using multiple CSPs.

### ⚫ Software-Defined Networks (SDNs): 
- SDNs utilize virtual network devices and services, including virtual switches, routers, and firewalls, often hosted on CSP servers, allowing for dynamic and programmable network configurations.

## 🔺 Benefits of Cloud Computing

### ⚫ Reliability: 
- Cloud computing offers consistent access to resources with minimal interruptions, ensuring high availability and secure connections.

### ⚫ Cost and Scalability: 
- CSPs reduce upfront costs by offering virtual services at a fraction of the price of maintaining on-premise infrastructure, and provide scalability through an elastic utility model where companies only pay for what they need.
