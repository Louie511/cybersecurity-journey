## 🔷 This lecture explains how Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) enhance network security by protecting your online privacy and data.

## 🔺 Understanding Internet Vulnerabilities

- When you connect to the internet, your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can see your network requests, which contain private information.
- This information, including your IP address and virtual location, could be intercepted, potentially linking your online activity to your physical location and personal details like bank accounts.

## 🔺 How VPNs Provide Security

- VPNs change your public IP address and hide your virtual location, making your data private when using public networks.
- They encrypt your data as it travels across the internet, ensuring confidentiality and making it unreadable to unauthorized parties.

## 🔺 Encapsulation and Encrypted Tunnels

- VPNs use a process called encapsulation, where sensitive data packets are wrapped within other data packets that routers can read.
- This allows your network requests to reach their destination while keeping your personal data encrypted and unreadable through an encrypted tunnel between your device and the VPN server.
