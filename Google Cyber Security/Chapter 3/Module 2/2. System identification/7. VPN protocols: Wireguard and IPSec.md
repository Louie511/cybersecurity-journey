## 🔷 This lecture explores Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), detailing their function, types, and the protocols they use to secure online communication.

## 🔺 Understanding VPNs

- A VPN is a network security service that masks your public IP address and encrypts data, ensuring privacy when using public networks.
- VPNs create a secure, virtual tunnel between your device and the internet, acting as a gateway to hide your IP address and encrypt data in transit.

## 🔺 Types of VPNs

### ⚫ Remote Access VPNs: 
- Used by individuals to establish a secure connection between a personal device and a VPN server, encrypting data sent or received.

### ⚫ Site-to-Site VPNs: 
- Employed by enterprises to extend their network across different locations, often using IPSec to create encrypted tunnels between networks.

## 🔺 VPN Protocols: WireGuard vs. IPSec

### ⚫ WireGuard VPN: 
- A newer, high-speed, and open-source protocol known for its advanced encryption, simpler setup, and enhanced download speeds due to fewer lines of code.

### ⚫ IPSec VPN: 
- An older, more complex protocol widely supported by operating systems, used by many VPN providers to encrypt and authenticate data packets, valued for its extensive security testing and widespread adoption.
