## 🔷 This lecture explains packet sniffing, a technique used by threat actors to gain unauthorized access to information, and how to protect against it.

## 🔺 Understanding Packet Sniffing

- Packet sniffing involves using software tools to observe data as it moves across a network, including valuable information like IP addresses, personal messages, and financial details.
- While security analysts use it for incident investigation and debugging, malicious actors can use it to spy on data not intended for them, similar to opening someone else's mail.

## 🔺 Types of Packet Sniffing Attacks

- **Passive packet sniffing** is when data packets are read in transit without manipulation, like a postal worker reading mail they are authorized to deliver but not to read.
- **Active packet sniffing** involves manipulating data packets in transit, such as altering information or redirecting packets, comparable to a neighbor intercepting and changing your mail.

## 🔺 Preventing Malicious Packet Sniffing

- Using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts data, making it unreadable to hackers even if they intercept traffic.
- Always ensure websites use HTTPS to encrypt data and prevent eavesdropping, especially on public networks.
- Avoid unprotected public Wi-Fi networks, as they lack encryption, making all data traveling to and from your device vulnerable to access by others on the network.
