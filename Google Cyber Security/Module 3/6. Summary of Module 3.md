## This summary provides key terms and definitions related to cybersecurity, focusing on concepts essential for understanding security policies and practices.

## Core Principles of Cybersecurity

### Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA) Triad: 
- This foundational model guides organizations in assessing risk when developing security systems and policies.

### Confidentiality: 
- Ensures that only authorized individuals can access specific assets or data.

## Data Protection and Compliance

### Privacy Protection: 
- The practice of safeguarding personal information from unauthorized use.

### Compliance: 
- Adhering to both internal organizational standards and external regulations, such as the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) for protecting health information.

## Security Frameworks and Controls

### Security Frameworks: 
- Guidelines, like the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, that help in creating plans to mitigate risks and threats to data and privacy.

### Security Controls: 
- Specific safeguards designed to reduce identified security risks within an organization's security architecture.
